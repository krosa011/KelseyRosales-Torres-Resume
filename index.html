Kelsey Rosales-Torres

Neuroscience



As a 4th year Neuroscience student at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), Kelsey has been involved in diverse experiences at UCR and in the community. At UCR, Kelsey is a Chancellor's Research Fellow working in a Behavior Neuroendocrinology lab with Dr. Wendy Saltzman. Kelsey studies paternal care in the biparental monogamous California mouse. Her current research focuses on immediate early gene expression in response to auditory and olfactory pup cues. Kelsey has attended and participated in confereneces in Southern California and hopes to publish a scientific article in the near future to conclude her current project.

Outside of her involvement at UCR, Kelsey works as a Resiliency Coach for a non-public agency, TTC4SUCCESS. Some of her tasks include: tutoring Special Education students, communicating with parents, teachers, and school administration, creating methods of service tracking via Google Sheets, and assisting with administrative tasks. She has a passion for working with Special Education students to ensure they are successful in the least restrictive educational environment. Moreover, she loves to collaborate to build a village of support for the students and families she works with.
